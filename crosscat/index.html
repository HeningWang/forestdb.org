CrossCat

CrossCat is a generative model that can be used for categorizing objects given their features. The model assumes that each data point is generated using multiple systems of categories; each such system accounts for some subset of the features (Shafto et al. 2006; Goodman et al. 2011).

(query

 (define kind-distribution (DPmem 1.0 gensym))

 (define feature->kind
   (mem (lambda (feature) (kind-distribution))))

 (define kind->class-distribution
   (mem (lambda (kind) (DPmem 1.0 gensym))))

 (define feature-kind/object->class
   (mem (lambda (kind object) (sample (kind->class-distribution kind)))))

 (define class->parameters
   (mem (lambda (object-class) (first (beta 1 1)))))

 (define (observe object feature)
   (flip (class->parameters (feature-kind/object->class (feature->kind feature) object))))

 (list (observe 'spinach 'breakfast)
       (observe 'eggs 'breakfast))

 (and (observe 'eggs 'breakfast)
      (observe 'toast 'breakfast)
      (observe 'eggs 'dinner)
      (observe 'spinach 'dinner)))

Goodman, Noah D., Joshua B. Tenenbaum, Timothy J. O’Donnell, and the Church Working Group. 2011. “Probabilistic Models of Cognition.” http://projects.csail.mit.edu/church/wiki/Church.

Shafto, P., Charles Kemp, Vikash Mansinghka, M. Gordon, and Joshua B. Tenenbaum. 2006. “Learning cross-cutting systems of categories.” Proceedings of the 28th Annual Conference of the Cognitive Science Society: 2146–2151.
